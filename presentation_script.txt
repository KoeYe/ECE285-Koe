──────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
SLIDE 1 — Title
──────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
我们研究的问题是 action-conditioned world modeling，
也就是给定当前帧和动作，预测下一帧。
我们用一个结构化的 latent token block 作为中间表示，
训练分三个阶段完成，整个模型只有 5.8M 参数，
在单卡上三小时以内能跑完。


──────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
SLIDE 2 — The Problem
──────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
核心问题是，之前的方法要么在像素空间里做预测，缺乏语义结构；
要么做纯 latent 的预测，但很难还原回像素。

我们的做法是中间路线：
用一个固定长度的 latent token block z-hat，
它既能被 Transformer predictor 生成，又能直接被 U-Net decoder 解码成像素。

公式：给定 (o_t, a_t)，先通过 f_theta 生成 z-hat，再通过 g_phi 解码成 o-hat。


──────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
SLIDE 3 — Architecture
──────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
模型由四个组件构成。

Vision Encoder：四层 CNN，把 64x64 输入帧编码成 64 个 patch token。
Action Encoder：把离散动作 L/D/R/U 嵌入一个 64 维向量。
Latent Predictor f_theta：4 层 Transformer，输入 encoder 输出和 action embedding，
  输出 K=8 个 d=128 的 latent token，共 1.5M 参数。
Pixel Decoder g_phi：U-Net 加 cross-attention，
  把 latent block 解码成 64x64 RGB 图，4M 参数。

总参数 5.8M，右边是数据流图。


──────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
SLIDE 4 — Three-Stage Training
──────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
训练分三个 stage，核心思路是把优化问题分解开来。

Stage 0：单独训练 Encoder 和 Decoder，做条件自编码，
  让 Decoder 先学会 FrozenLake 的视觉域。
  20 个 epoch，PSNR 31.3 dB，这是后续所有比较的 baseline。

Stage 1：冻结 Encoder 和 Decoder，只训练 Latent Predictor，
  目标是让 z-hat 对齐 Encoder 编出来的真实 latent z*。
  15 个 epoch，cosine similarity 0.997，latent 格式对齐。

Stage 2：端到端联合训练，
  loss = 像素重建 loss + lambda * embedding 正则，lambda=0.1。
  30 个 epoch，最终 PSNR 34.66 dB。


──────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
SLIDE 5 — Experimental Setup
──────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
环境是 FrozenLake-v1，8x8 网格，确定性，
我们自己写了 64x64 RGB 渲染器，
格子颜色：冰面蓝，洞黑，目标绿，agent 橙。

数据：200 个随机地图，每张 750 个 random rollout，
约 148K transition，按 160/20/20 分 train/val/test。
硬件：单卡 NVIDIA L40S。

选 FrozenLake 的原因：确定性环境出现预测误差一定是模型问题，
不是环境随机性；ablation 干净；整个流程不到 3 小时。


──────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
SLIDE 6 — Main Results
──────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
主要结果：
PSNR 34.66 dB，比 Stage 0 baseline 高 3.36 dB。
SSIM 0.9959，结构相似度接近完美。
Drift_10 = 0.02341，连续自回归 10 步之后误差依然很小。

右边说明了数字含义：
PSNR > 35 dB 在 64x64 上像素误差几乎不可见；
Drift_10 < 0.006 说明多步 rollout 质量持续稳定。

关键机制：embedding loss 在 feature space 施加约束，
不只是对齐像素，这是 rollout 稳定的核心原因。

这是多步 rollout 可视化，每行三张：GT / 预测 / 误差图（x10 放大）。
没有任何 GT 反馈，纯自回归。

SLIDE 8 — Ablation Study
──────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
四组 ablation：

No Stage 0：PSNR 直接崩到 9.45 dB。decoder 预适应是必须的。

Lambda：
  lambda=0，PSNR 接近（34.58），但 Drift_5 比默认高 65%，多步不稳。
  lambda=1.0，过约束，PSNR 降到 32.23。lambda=0.1 最优。

K（latent block 长度）：
  K=2: 33.50，K=4: 33.94，K=16: 34.34，K=8: 34.66。K=8 是 sweet spot。

部分解冻 decoder：PSNR 虚高到 49.57，
  但那是 decoder 把真实 latent 记忆化了，退化解，不是 world model。
  冻住 decoder 是归纳偏置，不是限制。

右边 drift 曲线：蓝线平稳，lambda=0 漂移，No Stage 0 立刻崩。

SLIDE 9 — Latent Space Analysis
──────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
PCA 可视化：2000 个 test sample，按 action 颜色标注。
四个 action 形成清晰分离的 cluster，
说明 latent predictor 学到了 action-conditioned 的结构，
没有任何显式 clustering 监督。

每个 cluster 内部有两个 sub-cloud，原因见下一张。


──────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
SLIDE 10 — Bimodal Latent Structure
──────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
每个 action cluster 分成两个 sub-cloud，对应两种物理情况：
一种是 agent 在墙边，动作无效，o_t = o_{t+1}，即 boundary no-op；
另一种是 agent 成功移动，observation 发生变化。

这是自然涌现的，完全没有显式监督：
decoder 对两种情况要产生不同的像素输出，
反向传播迫使 latent predictor 在 latent space 把两者分开。

数据里约 24.7% 的 transition 是 boundary no-op，
比例很高，所以模型必须学会区分它们。


──────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
SLIDE 11 — Key Insights
──────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
三个关键 insight：

Insight 2，Embedding loss 创造稳定吸引子：
  PSNR 从 t+1 到 t+8 快速下降后趋于平稳。
  去掉 embedding loss，Drift_5 恶化 65%。
  feature space 约束比单纯像素约束更有效。

Insight 3，冻结 decoder 是归纳偏置：
  解冻之后 PSNR 虚高到 49.57，但那是 lookup table，不是 world model。
  冻住迫使 f_theta 学习真正的 transition 规律。

Insight 4，阶段式训练是优化分解：
  直接端到端从头训练，9.45 dB，崩了。
  三阶段分别缩小搜索空间，让每步都能收敛。


──────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
SLIDE 12 — Stage 3: Planning
──────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
最后一个实验：world model 能否帮助下游规划？

设置：20 个 held-out 地图，每张 5 个 episode，最多 80 步。
VLM 用 Qwen2-VL-2B，在 20K 个 BFS 标注的 transition 上做了 LoRA 微调（r=16）。

WM 接入方式叫 lookahead-consistency filter：
  1. VLM 给出当前帧的首选 action
  2. WM 对全部 4 个 action 各预测一个未来帧
  3. VLM 从每个预测帧再推理一次动作
  4. 剔除那些预测结果让 VLM 想立刻反向的 action
  5. 在剩余候选里返回 VLM 的首选

结果：VLM-only 5%，VLM+WM 5%，Oracle BFS 100%，两者持平。

原因：单步 lookahead 等价于在 WM latent 空间做 greedy search，
遇到局部死角同样无法恢复。reversal filter 触发概率很低，
大多数 action 直接通过，结果退化成 VLM-only。

结论：WM 预测质量高（34.66 dB）不自动带来规划增益，
需要多步 rollout 加 value function。


──────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
SLIDE 13 — Conclusion
──────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
总结：
三阶段 latent world model，5.8M 参数，单卡 3 小时内训完。
PSNR 34.66 dB，SSIM 0.9959，多步 rollout 稳定。

两个关键设计：
阶段式训练做优化分解；embedding 正则保持 rollout 稳定。

规划实验说明：高质量预测不自动转化为规划增益。

Future work：scheduled sampling，stochastic 环境，
contrastive embedding 目标，多步 rollout 加 value function。

──────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
SLIDE 1 — Title
──────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
We work on action-conditioned world modeling —
given the current observation and an action, predict the next frame.
Our key idea is to use a structured latent token block as an intermediate state carrier.
Training is split into three stages, the full model has 5.8 million parameters,
and it trains from scratch on a single GPU in under three hours.


──────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
SLIDE 2 — The Problem
──────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
Prior work sits at two extremes.
Pixel-space models directly predict RGB frames but have no semantic structure.
Pure latent models work in abstract spaces that are hard to decode back to pixels.

We take a middle path: a fixed-length latent token block z-hat
that is both generated by a Transformer predictor and directly decodable
by a U-Net decoder into pixel predictions.

The inference chain is: (o_t, a_t) → f_theta → z-hat → g_phi → o-hat_{t+1}.


──────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
SLIDE 3 — Architecture
──────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
The model has four components.

The Vision Encoder is a four-layer CNN that encodes a 64x64 input frame
into 64 patch tokens of dimension 64.

The Action Encoder embeds each discrete action — left, down, right, up —
into a 64-dimensional vector.

The Latent Predictor f_theta is a 4-layer Transformer.
It takes the encoder output and action embedding,
and outputs K=8 latent tokens of dimension 128. This is 1.5M parameters.

The Pixel Decoder g_phi is a U-Net with a cross-attention bottleneck.
It takes the latent block and decodes it to a 64x64 RGB image. That's 4M parameters.

Total: 5.8M parameters. The right side shows the data flow.


──────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
SLIDE 4 — Three-Stage Training
──────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
Training is decomposed into three stages, each targeting a different subproblem.

Stage 0 trains the Encoder and Decoder together as a conditional autoencoder,
so the decoder learns the FrozenLake visual domain before anything else.
20 epochs, PSNR reaches 31.3 dB. This is our baseline for all comparisons.

Stage 1 freezes the Encoder and Decoder and trains only the Latent Predictor,
aligning its output z-hat to match the encoder's real latent z-star.
15 epochs, cosine similarity reaches 0.997. The latent format is now stabilized.

Stage 2 trains end-to-end with a combined pixel reconstruction loss
plus an embedding regularization term, lambda=0.1.
30 epochs. Final PSNR: 34.66 dB.


──────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
SLIDE 5 — Experimental Setup
──────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
The environment is FrozenLake-v1, 8x8 grid, deterministic.
We wrote a custom 64x64 RGB renderer: ice tiles are blue, holes black,
the goal green, and the agent orange.

Dataset: 200 random map seeds, 750 random-action rollouts per map,
about 148K transitions total, split 160/20/20 for train/val/test.
Hardware: single NVIDIA L40S GPU.

We chose FrozenLake because it's deterministic — any prediction error
is purely a model failure, not environmental noise —
and because ablations are fast and clean. The whole pipeline runs in under 3 hours.


──────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
SLIDE 6 — Main Results
──────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
Our full model achieves:
PSNR 34.66 dB, which is +3.36 dB over the Stage 0 baseline.
SSIM 0.9959, near-perfect structural similarity.
Drift_10 = 0.02341, meaning quality stays high over 10 auto-regressive steps.

The right panel puts these numbers in context:
at 64x64 resolution, PSNR above 35 dB means pixel errors are essentially invisible.
Drift_10 below 0.006 means the model remains stable over long rollouts.

The embedding loss is the key mechanism — it penalizes drift in feature space,
not just at the pixel level, and that's what makes multi-step rollouts stable.

SLIDE 7 — Rollout Visualization
──────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
This shows multi-step rollout outputs. Each row has three images:

For steps 1 through 8, the agent position and tile colors are tracked accurately.

SLIDE 8 — Ablation Study
──────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
We ran four ablation sweeps, each for 5 epochs.

No Stage 0: PSNR collapses to 9.45 dB.
The decoder has no visual prior; training diverges immediately.

Lambda, the embedding loss weight:
  lambda=0 gives similar one-step PSNR (34.58) but Drift_5 is 65% worse.
  lambda=1.0 over-constrains and drops PSNR to 32.23.
  lambda=0.1 is optimal.

K, the latent block length:
  K=2: 33.50, K=4: 33.94, K=16: 34.34, K=8: 34.66.
  K=8 is the sweet spot.

Partial decoder unfreeze: PSNR inflates to 49.57 dB.
  This is a degenerate solution — the output layers memorize the rendering function
  rather than learning a general world model.
  Freezing the decoder is an intentional inductive bias.

The drift curves on the right show: the full model is stable,
lambda=0 drifts, and No Stage 0 collapses immediately.

SLIDE 9 — Latent Space Analysis
──────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
This is a PCA visualization of the predicted latents z-hat,
using 2000 test samples colored by action.

Four clearly separated clusters emerge, one per action.
This means the latent predictor has learned action-conditioned structure
with no explicit clustering supervision.

But each cluster splits into two sub-clouds. What does that mean? Next slide.


──────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
SLIDE 10 — Bimodal Latent Structure
──────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
Each action cluster splits into two sub-clouds corresponding to two physical cases.

The first is a boundary no-op: the agent is at a wall,
the action has no effect, and o_t equals o_{t+1}.

The second is an effective transition: the agent moves,
and the observation shifts by one cell.

This structure emerges without any explicit supervision.
The decoder must produce different pixel outputs for the two cases,
so backpropagation forces the latent predictor to separate them in latent space.

About 24.7% of test transitions are boundary no-ops — a large fraction —
so the model has strong pressure to make this distinction.


──────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
SLIDE 11 — Key Insights
──────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
Three key insights from our analysis.

Insight 2 — Embedding loss creates a stable attractor:
  PSNR drops from t+1 to t+8, then plateaus.
  Without the embedding term, Drift_5 worsens by 65%.
  Feature-space regularization is more effective than pixel loss alone.

Insight 3 — Frozen decoder is an inductive bias, not a limitation:
  Unfreezing inflates PSNR to 49.57, but the model degenerates into a lookup table.
  Freezing forces f_theta to learn general transition dynamics.

Insight 4 — Staged training is optimization decomposition:
  End-to-end training from scratch collapses to 9.45 dB.
  Each stage narrows the search space for the next,
  making the overall optimization tractable.


──────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
SLIDE 12 — Stage 3: Planning
──────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
The final experiment asks: does world model quality translate to planning performance?

Setup: 20 held-out maps, 5 episodes each, maximum 80 steps per episode.
We fine-tuned Qwen2-VL-2B with LoRA (r=16) on 20K BFS-labeled transitions
to serve as both the baseline policy and the evaluator.

The WM is integrated via a lookahead-consistency filter:
  Step 1: VLM picks a primary action from the current frame.
  Step 2: WM predicts the next frame for all 4 possible actions.
  Step 3: VLM is re-queried from each of those 4 predicted frames.
  Step 4: Actions whose predicted future elicits an immediate reversal are pruned.
  Step 5: Return the VLM's primary choice among the surviving actions.

Results: VLM-only 5%, VLM+WM 5%, Oracle BFS 100%. Both methods tie.

Why? Single-step lookahead is equivalent to greedy search in the WM's latent space.
It can still walk into dead ends with no way to backtrack.
The reversal filter rarely triggers — most actions pass through —
so the behavior degenerates to VLM-only.

The takeaway: high prediction quality (34.66 dB PSNR) does not automatically
translate to planning gains. Multi-step rollouts with a value function are needed.


──────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
SLIDE 13 — Conclusion
──────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
To summarize:

We built a three-stage latent world model.
5.8M parameters, trained from scratch on a single GPU in under 3 hours.
PSNR 34.66 dB, SSIM 0.9959, stable multi-step rollouts.

Two design choices are critical:
staged training as optimization decomposition,
and embedding regularization for rollout stability.

The planning experiment shows that prediction quality and planning utility
are distinct: high fidelity alone is not sufficient.

Future directions include scheduled sampling to reduce exposure bias,
evaluation on stochastic and continuous environments,
contrastive embedding objectives,
and multi-step WM rollouts paired with a learned value function.

Q&A Reference (both versions)
===================================================================================

Q: Why not use a larger model?
A: 5.8M parameters is an intentional constraint to validate the staged training
   strategy rather than relying on scale. Scaling is orthogonal to the contribution.

Q: Why is VLM success rate only 5%?
A: FrozenLake requires navigating around holes without a map prior.
   The LoRA fine-tuning is on random-action trajectories, not optimal paths,
   so the VLM itself is a weak baseline by design.

Q: PSNR is high — why didn't planning improve?
A: Accurate next-frame prediction does not encode which path leads to the goal.
   Greedy single-step lookahead is equivalent to asking the VLM directly.
   Multi-step rollout with a value function is needed to escape local dead ends.

Q: Isn't FrozenLake too simple?
A: Yes — that's explicitly stated in Limitations. Determinism and small state space
   make ablations clean. Stochastic and continuous environments are future work.

Q: How was K=8 chosen?
A: Ablation across K=2/4/8/16. K=8 is the PSNR sweet spot;
   K=16 adds minimal gain at higher compute cost.

Q: How is the Stage 1 cosine similarity of 0.997 measured?
A: For every transition in the validation set, we compute the cosine similarity
   between f_theta's output z-hat and the encoder's true latent z-star,
   then average. 0.997 confirms directional alignment;
   Stage 2's pixel loss handles any residual scale mismatch.
